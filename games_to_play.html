1 Games I own that need playing

1.1 Games I have played 0 times

  1. Pathogenesis – This is a microbiology-themed deck builder with some interesting semi-coop mechanics
  2. Tribes: Early Civilization – This is a sort of prequel from the people who made Nations (another game I haven't played). Not too heavy, not too long, resource gathering with some blocking mechanics.
  3. Empires of the Void II – Eclipse-like game by the fellow who made Near and Far. Less aggro than Eclipse, lots of resource management.
  4. Pixie Queen – Worker placement, action drafting, resource management, somewhat punishing in the sense that much of the work is to avoid getting negative points in a round.
  5. Tramways – A map-building, train game by the guy who did Age of Steam
  6. Iberian Rails – Stocks in train companies with an interesting action mechanic that involves selecting an avatar for the round and using the avatar's specific abilities to lay rail and buy stock.
  7. Isaribi – A fishing game by the fellow who did Trains, Sail to India, and the amazing Yokohama.
  8. Underlings of Underwing – Worker placement and control of a changing play tableau. Also lots of pretty, colored acrylic gems.

1.2 Games I have played not enough times to have an opinion

  1. Deus Ex Machina – Play as Greek god inspiring heroes to fight mythical monsters. (~90 min)
  2. Vast: The Crystal Caverns – A very asymmetric dungeon crawler where each player runs a character with completely different mechanics. Basically 2 to 5 independent games played simultaneously. (2 - 3 hours)
  3. Bethel Woods – Coop, thematic, puzzle solving game by the fellow who make Raiders of the North Sea. (~60 min)
  4. Lisboa – One of the best games I own, but long and complicated. It's not quite an area control game, not quite a tableau management game, not quite a tile placement game. Has a complex, deeply interconnected economy. I have played 3 times and want/need many more. (3 hours)
